# ART-006: Sex Appeal and Damage-Art Policy

**Summary:** Production rules for how far `Shogun` should push sex appeal, how FEH-style damage art should be interpreted, and what animation choices are too risky for a store-safe collectible RPG.

## Purpose

Use this note when deciding:

- how sexy `Shogun` should be overall
- what kind of female-character fanservice is acceptable
- how damage art should work
- what battle-animation choices are safe versus risky
- where to draw the line between collectible allure and softcore bait

## Executive rule

`Shogun` should aim for:

- **stylized sexy**
- **alluring, elegant, dangerous, or romantic**
- **suggestive rather than explicit**

It should avoid:

- porn-forward framing
- fetish-forward defeat art
- sexualized humiliation
- obvious body-part spectacle as the main selling point

The target is closer to `Fire Emblem Heroes` than to a more aggressively erotic gacha style.

## Why this line exists

This project wants:

- strong collectible appeal
- broad store survivability
- a roster that feels desirable without feeling cheap

That means sex appeal should function as:

- an enhancer of character fantasy
- an amplifier of rarity and collectibility
- a contrast tool in damage art and variants

It should **not** become the only identity of the game.

## FEH pattern worth learning from

The reviewed `Fire Emblem Heroes` examples show a repeatable pattern:

1. female units are often glamorous and suggestive in the base art
2. damage art increases exposure through torn fabric, asymmetry, and loss of composure
3. the character still reads as a complete fantasy archetype, not just exposed body parts
4. the compositions remain mostly full-body and character-led
5. the result is fanservice with plausible battle framing, not overt pornography

This is the useful lesson:

- sexualize through archetype, costume, pose, and contrast
- do not sexualize through explicit sexual action or humiliation logic

## Acceptable sex-appeal zone

### Good targets

- cleavage
- side cutouts
- high slits
- sheer fabrics
- thigh emphasis
- elegant hip lines
- exposed shoulders, backs, or stomachs
- seductive expressions
- glamorous costumes with strong silhouette identity

### Good emotional tones

- elegant
- seductive
- teasing
- dangerous
- tragic
- proud
- vulnerable-but-dignified

### Good contexts

- dancers
- shrine or ritual elegance
- noble or courtly glamour
- beach or summer variants
- dreamlike or supernatural temptation
- ceremonial or festival attire

## Red-line zone

Avoid:

- explicit nudity
- obvious porn framing
- fetish framing around pain, tears, or helplessness
- sexualized childlike or youthful-looking characters
- "clothes exploded off" logic
- sexual content whose main function is gratification rather than character fantasy
- repeated camera or composition focus that exists mainly to leer at breasts, ass, or crotch

If the player can describe the image mainly as "gooner bait," the design has gone too far.

## Damage-art rule

Damage art is allowed and can be strategically useful, but it must read as:

- battle-worn glamour
- vulnerability
- loss of composure
- tension
- character contrast

It must **not** read as:

- sexualized harm
- humiliation porn
- fetishized pain
- coercive or assault-coded imagery

### Good damage-art cues

- torn sleeves, hems, veils, capes, stockings, or sashes
- loosened hair
- cracked accessories
- changed posture
- a more vulnerable or strained expression
- slightly increased skin exposure through damage

### Bad damage-art cues

- moaning or orgasmic expression
- crying framed erotically
- bondage-coded helplessness
- exposure that feels engineered rather than caused by battle motion
- repeated focus on breasts or crotch during the damage state

The goal is:

- "she looks shaken, exposed, and more vulnerable"

Not:

- "this is secretly softcore defeat art"

## Animation rule

Battle animation should stay readable first and sexy second.

### Allowed

- subtle full-body sensuality
- hip sway that still reads as character motion
- cloth, hair, sleeve, and cape follow-through
- natural secondary motion
- elegant or flirtatious attack poses if the full animation still reads as combat

### Not recommended

- deliberate boob-bounce systems
- obvious ass-jiggle as a selling point
- attack animations whose purpose is to spotlight sexual body motion
- repeated breast or hip oscillation that a reviewer would immediately describe as fanservice tech

If someone can summarize the animation as "the boobs bounce" or "the ass jiggles," it is too close to the line.

## Roster-balance rule

Do not make every female character sexy in the same way.

Use varied forms of appeal:

- noble beauty
- mature glamour
- dangerous seduction
- tragic softness
- mystical elegance
- athletic confidence
- fierce antihero energy

This matters because:

- variety increases collection appeal
- repetition makes the roster feel cheap and forgettable
- archetype diversity gives future variants more room to breathe

## Implementation rule for Shogun

Use sex appeal differently by asset lane.

### Battle assets

- keep readability first
- keep exposure moderate
- no body-part-focused animation gimmicks
- sensuality through pose, silhouette, and styling

### Collection assets

- can push glamour and allure further
- portraits, busts, reveal art, and banner art can be more sensual than gameplay sprites
- still stay inside the "suggestive, not explicit" lane

### Damage art

- use selectively on high-value adult characters
- strongest use case is contrast against a composed normal state
- must preserve dignity and archetype identity

## FEH-inspired design takeaway

The part worth borrowing from FEH is:

- glamorous full-body composition
- sexy but mostly character-led design
- damage-state contrast
- seasonal/contextual permission to push further

The part not worth copying is:

- escalating toward explicit jiggle logic
- making sexual defeat the main emotional hook
- flattening all female appeal into the same breast-forward template

## Platform and rating constraint

This policy exists partly because store policy matters.

Current constraints:

- Apple bans overtly sexual or pornographic material intended to stimulate erotic feelings
- Google Play bans content intended to be sexually gratifying and is especially sensitive to explicit sexualized depictions of body parts or animated sexual content

That means `Shogun` should stay in a broad `suggestive / teen-safe` zone rather than trying to game the line with obvious erotic animation.

## Decision rule

When unsure, ask:

1. does this make the character more collectible, or just more naked?
2. does this still read as character fantasy first?
3. would this survive store review without feeling embarrassing to defend?
4. would the game still feel coherent if several units used this level of fanservice?

If the answer to those questions is weak, scale it back.
