Game Design Document (GDD) for "Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood" (BRIEF)


  

1. Concept Summary
* Game Title: "Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood"
* Brief Description: "Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood" is an innovative mobile game that blends the engaging mechanics of 'Ninja Blazing' with the artistic flair of 'Ronin: The Last Samurai.' It combines the strategy and depth of classic martial arts with a modern interactive experience. Designed for portability and accessibility on mobile devices, it offers a deeply strategic, skill-based gameplay experience. Players can enjoy both solo and multiplayer modes, emphasizing precision, timing, and tactical depth. "Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood" is not just another gacha game; it's a visually striking, replayable game crafted to appeal to both casual gamers and hardcore enthusiasts.
2. Core Gameplay Mechanics
* Combat System: The combat emphasizes player skill and timing, featuring gesture-based controls for sword fighting and special abilities. Players must master tapping, pressing, and swiping mechanics to effectively combat enemies, drawing inspiration from successful mechanics in 'Mortal Kombat Mobile.' The core of the gameplay involves precise control over character movement and attack ranges. Movement is free-form on the battlefield (not limited to a radius or circle), only restricted by obstacles and battlefield boundaries. Each character has a fixed attack range (short, mid, or long), visualized as a circle around the unit. Attacks are only possible if the target is within this circle. If a unit ends its turn within the attack range of an enemy, that enemy can perform a basic attack (counterattack) on the unit, even if not directly targeted. Multiple enemies can attack if the player ends their turn within multiple enemy ranges.
* Range and Movement Mechanics: Inspired by 'Naruto Ninja Blazing,' each character in "Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood" possesses a unique attack range depicted as a circle around them, which dictates the reach of their jutsu or attacks. Movement is not limited to a circle; it is free-form within the battlefield boundaries. Some characters might excel in close combat with smaller, more potent ranges, while others might specialize in long-range attacks.
* Combo System: Elemental synergies and weapon-type combos are inspired by "The Book of Five Rings," with players exploiting strengths and weaknesses inherent to different elements and weapons. This system encourages strategic planning and execution to maximize effectiveness in battle. Combos can be initiated when characters within compatible range circles perform synchronized attacks, offering powerful effects against adversaries.
* Environmental Interactions: The game includes dynamic environments that players can use to gain tactical advantages or that can change the course of battle. Features like destructible cover (trees, boulders) and elemental hazards (rivers affecting elemental powers, poison fogs) add layers of strategic depth. The impact of environmental interactions also varies depending on the character's range, with certain environmental features amplifying or reducing the effectiveness of character abilities based on their proximity and interaction.
This enhancement emphasizes the importance of strategic positioning and movement, making the gameplay experience more dynamic and tactical. Players will need to consider both the range and the special abilities of their characters to excel in combat, reflecting a deeper layer of strategy and player engagement.
3. Progression and Customization
* Skill Trees: Players can develop their characters through expansive skill trees that offer unique abilities and combat styles. This includes the potential integration of combat postures or stances, providing diverse gameplay experiences and strategic choices.
* Character Customization: Simplified customization options allow for visual and functional changes without overwhelming players. The focus is on enhancing the game's strategic elements while allowing players to personalize their characters' appearances and gear to a degree.
4. Narrative
* Story Integration: The narrative is dark and cinematic, centered around an alliance of samurai, ninja, and ronin who must unite against a demonic invasion. The story adapts to player decisions, influencing both the immediate gameplay environment and broader story outcomes.
* Cultural and Historical Elements: The game delves deeply into Japanese mythology and history, featuring samurai lore, the strategic teachings of Miyamoto Musashi, and elements from feudal Japan. This rich cultural tapestry adds depth and authenticity to the game world.
5. Monetization Strategy
* Model: Utilizing a gacha system for character acquisition, complemented by a seasonal pass that offers rewards tied to gameplay achievements. The in-game currency will be visually thematic, enhancing the overall game experience and fitting seamlessly within the game's world.
6. Community Engagement
* Updates and Communication: An active, managed community on platforms like Discord will be central to gathering player feedback and driving regular content updates.
* Feedback Mechanisms: Closed beta testing and special preview events for community influencers will help refine the game, ensuring a balanced and engaging experience while protecting the game's unique elements from potential theft.
Development and Release Strategy
* The development phase will focus on creating a strong prototype to facilitate crowdfunding efforts. Community feedback will be integral during the beta testing phase to refine gameplay and ensure a balanced, engaging experience.

SECTION 2: CORE GAMEPLAY SYSTEMS


2.1 Combat Philosophy
Shogun: Flowers Fall in Blood aims to merge the thoughtful precision of turn-based tactics with the kinetic feedback of gesture execution.
Every action should feel intentional. Every misstep should cost the player.
Design Goal:
Players must balance strategic positioning, team synergy, and real-time gesture skill execution within the constraints of turn economy and battlefield layouts.


2.2 Battlefield Structure

2.2.2 Zones
                     * Frontline: Melee warriors, tanks

                     * Midline: Flexible attackers, supports

                     * Backline: Archers, mystics, ranged units

2.2.3 Tile Properties
                        * Empty

                        * Obstacle (tree, stone lantern, wreckage; blocks movement and some attacks)

                        * Hazard (poison patch, fire ground, electrified zone; causes status effects or damage over time)



2.3 Unit Movement System
                           * Each unit has a Movement Radius (shown visually)

                           * Movement cost is 1 tile = 1 movement point

                           * Default: 2–3 tiles per turn depending on unit type

                           * Units cannot move through obstacles or other units

Movement decisions consume the Move Action of the turn.


2.4 Turn-Based Action System
2.4.1 Turn Order
                              * Initiative stat (SPD) determines action order

                              * High SPD units can act earlier and more often

                              * Turn sequence shown via a visible turn queue

2.4.2 Action Types
A unit may perform one action per turn, after (or before) movement:
                                 * Basic Attack (tap gesture)

                                 * Skill Attack (swipe or hold gesture)

                                 * Use Item

                                 * Defend (reduce incoming damage by 50%)

                                 * Pass Turn

Units may also choose to move first, then act or act first, then move (flexible turn resolution).

The player learns mastery through repetition, creating a true "skill expression layer" on top of traditional RPG stat combat.


2.6 Range & Targeting Mechanics
                                       * Units have an Attack Radius

                                       * Radius is visualized during targeting phase

                                       * Melee units: radius = adjacent tiles

                                       * Ranged units: radius = up to 5 tiles

                                       * Attacks cannot pass through solid obstacles

Example:

                                             * Monk + Onmyoji: "Purification Ritual" (damage + cleanse debuffs)

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Higher rarity units require more EXP to level up

                                                                                                                                                                                          * Requires rare Awakening Materials (boss drop + shop item)

                                                                                                                                                                                          * Unlocks visual upgrades and new abilities

6.3.3 Limit Breaking
                                                                                                                                                                                             * Maxed out characters (Lv.100) can be limit broken +1 to +5 times

                                                                                                                                                                                             * Requires Limit Break Crystals (super rare materials)

                                                                                                                                                                                             * Provides % stat boosts and potentially unlocks secondary passives at final stages

6.3.4 Gear Progression
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Weapons, Armor, Accessories can be enhanced up to +15 levels

                                                                                                                                                                                                * Gear can also be evolved from Common ➔ Relic quality tiers

                                                                                                                                                                                                * Special relic equipment may offer unique skill modifiers or visual VFX

6.3.5 Weapon Mastery
                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Units earn Mastery XP with specific weapons

                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Unlocks small permanent buffs + special finishers tied to weapon type

13.2 Core Combat Formula Examples
13.2.1 Damage Calculation
Base Formula:
 (Attacker ATK × Skill Multiplier × Element Modifier) - Target DEF = Raw Damage
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * Element Modifier = 1.25 (advantage), 0.75 (disadvantage), 1.0 (neutral)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * Minimum damage floor exists to prevent 0 output

13.2.2 Critical Hit Formula
If (Random roll ≤ CRIT RATE) → Damage × CRIT DAMAGE multiplier
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Default CRIT Damage = 1.5x base hit

13.2.3 Status Chance Application
If (Random roll ≤ Skill Status Chance - Target RESISTANCE %) → Apply Status Effect
